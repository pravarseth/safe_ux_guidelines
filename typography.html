Typography

Typography

Typefaces

Our design guidelines aims to reduce the friction between people and their tools and to ultimately help them to achieve their goals. To accomplish this across our brand, we’ve chosen a simple and familiar typeface.

Open Sans - a sans serif typeface designed by Steve Matteson, Type Director of Ascender Corp. It was optimised for print, web, and mobile interfaces, and has excellent legibility characteristics in its letterforms.

Font styles

In our design guidelines we recommend 7 styles:

  • 4 bold styles to be used in headers, form labels and bold statements.
  • 3 regular styles to be used for copy and form inputs.
  • Each style covers same typeface with its own weight, size, line-height and letter-spacing specifications. And also what sort of scenarios to use each style.

Black 1

  • Should be used only once in the screen/page and text should be a line.
  • Example in a splash screen or login screen.

class-name: text-black-1

font-size: 48px

font-weight: bold

line-spacing: 54px

Example

<h1 class="text-black-1">The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog</h1>

Black 2

  • Should be used only once in the screen/page and text should be a line.
  • Example in a page heading.

class-name: text-black-2

font-size: 36px

font-weight: bold

line-spacing: 40px

Example

<h1 class="text-black-2">The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog</h1>

Bold 1

  • Text should be a line and can be used multiple times in a page/screen.
  • Example in a heading for a paragraph.

class-name: text-bold-1

font-size: 30px

font-weight: semi-bold

line-spacing: 33px

Example

<h2 class="text-bold-1">The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog</h2>

Bold 2

  • Text should be a line and can be used multiple times in a page/screen
  • Example in a heading for a copy.

class-name: text-bold-2

font-size: 24px

font-weight: semi-bold

line-spacing: 27px

Example

<h2 class="text-bold-2">The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog</h2>

Regular 1

  • Special use case style for copy and form inputs on large screens.

class-name: text-normal-1

font-size: 18px

font-weight: regular

line-spacing: 20px

Example

<p class="text-normal-1">The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog</p>

Regular 2

  • Primary style for all body copy and form inputs.

class-name: text-normal-2

font-size: 16px

font-weight: regular

line-spacing: 18px

Example

<p class="text-normal-2">The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog</p>

Regular 3
  • Used as small print.
  • Examples include a note, starred item, label on input focus or an ID code.

class-name: text-normal-3

font-size: 14px

font-weight: regular

line-spacing: 16px

Example

<p class="text-normal-3">The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog</p>

Development

Guides to extend SCSS library:

  • Typography related variables are found within scss/support/typography.scss.
  • Every typography related class must prefix with text (Use variable $typo-prefix).
  • Most of the variables are provided as SCSS Maps which make it easy to extend.
